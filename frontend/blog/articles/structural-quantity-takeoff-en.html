Structural Quantity Take-off: Methods and Best Practices

January 8, 2026 14 min read

🏗️ Main Structural Works

Structural works that need to be taken off include:

  • Excavation and backfill
  • Plain and reinforced concrete
  • Reinforcement steel
  • Formwork
  • Waterproofing and compaction

📐 Concrete Take-off

Basic Formula:

Concrete Volume (m³) = Length × Width × Height

Concrete Elements Take-off:

ElementCalculation MethodNotes
Isolated FootingsLength × Width × DepthEach footing separately
Raft FoundationArea × ThicknessDeduct staircase openings
Columns(Cross-section) × HeightFrom slab to slab
BeamsWidth × Depth × LengthDeduct part inside slab
SlabsArea × ThicknessDeduct beam areas

Concrete Wastage:

Usually add 3-5% to compensate for loss during pouring and transport.

🔩 Reinforcement Take-off

Calculation Method:

  1. Read Bar Bending Schedule (BBS).
  2. Calculate each bar length (including Hooks and Laps).
  3. Multiply length × standard weight per linear meter.

General Formula:

Weight (kg) = (d² / 162) × Length (m)
where d = bar diameter in mm

Lapping Lengths:

  • Tension: 40-50 × bar diameter
  • Compression: 30-40 × bar diameter

Reinforcement Wastage:

Usually add 3-5% for cutting loss and splicing requirements.

📦 Formwork Take-off

Formwork Concept:

Formwork is temporary molds where concrete is poured. Measured in square meters (m²).

Formwork Area Calculation:

ElementCalculation Method
FootingsPerimeter × Depth + Bottom area
ColumnsPerimeter × Height
Beams(Bottom + 2 Sides) × Length
SlabsBottom area + Edges

Number of Uses:

  • Regular timber: 3-5 times
  • Plywood: 5-8 times
  • Steel formwork: 50-100 times

⛏️ Excavation Take-off

Excavation Volume (m³) = Length × Width × Depth
+ Working Space from each side (usually 50-100 cm)

Bulking Factor:

Soil after excavation increases in volume:

  • Sandy soil: 1.10 - 1.15
  • Clay soil: 1.20 - 1.30
  • Rock: 1.40 - 1.60

💡 Professional Tips

  • Use organized Excel sheets for each element.
  • Check calculations using different method (total vs detailed).
  • Don't forget connection and anchoring elements.
  • Use CSI codes for item classification.
