Quantity Take-off (or Material Take-off) is the process of extracting material quantities and work items from engineering drawings and specifications. This process is fundamental for preparing the Bill of Quantities (BOQ) and accurately pricing the project.

üìä Types of Take-off

1. Approximate Take-off

Used in early project stages to estimate total cost:

  • Based on gross areas and estimation factors.
  • Low accuracy (¬±25-30%).
  • Fast and doesn't require detailed drawings.

2. Detailed Take-off

Used when submitting bids and executing projects:

  • Based on careful reading of every drawing.
  • High accuracy (¬±5-10%).
  • Takes considerable time and requires expertise.

üîß Detailed Take-off Steps

  1. Study Drawings: Understand the entire project before starting.
  2. Identify Items: Divide work according to BOQ or WBS.
  3. Measure: Use scale rulers or software (AutoCAD, Bluebeam).
  4. Calculate: Apply correct engineering formulas.
  5. Review: Check calculations at least twice.

üíª Modern Take-off Tools

  • Bluebeam Revu: For measuring from PDF files.
  • PlanSwift: Specialized take-off software.
  • CostX: 2D and 3D take-off.
  • Revit: Automatic take-off from BIM models.

üí° Tips for Accurate Take-off

  • Use the same units throughout all calculations.
  • Document the source of every number (sheet number, detail).
  • Check take-off using different method (e.g., calculate from two directions).
  • Don't forget hidden elements (foundations, installations).
