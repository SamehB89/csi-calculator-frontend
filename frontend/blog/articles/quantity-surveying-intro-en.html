Introduction to Quantity Surveying

January 8, 2026 10 min read

üìê What is Quantity Surveying?

Quantity Surveying is the art and science of extracting material quantities and work items from engineering drawings and specifications. It's the cornerstone for project pricing, bid preparation, and cost control during execution.

üë∑ Role of the Quantity Surveyor (QS)

The Quantity Surveyor (QS) is the specialist in construction cost management. Their duties include:

  • Pre-Contract Phase:
    • Preparing preliminary cost estimates
    • Taking off quantities from drawings
    • Preparing Bill of Quantities (BOQ)
    • Analyzing and comparing bids
  • Post-Contract Phase:
    • Measuring executed works
    • Preparing monthly interim payments
    • Evaluating change orders
    • Preparing final account

üìä Importance of Accurate Take-off

  • For Owner: Know expected cost before starting.
  • For Contractor: Accurate pricing ensures profit and avoids loss.
  • For Consultant: Basis for comparing bids.
  • For Financing: Banks need reliable estimates.

üìã Bill of Quantities (BOQ)

BOQ is the official document containing:

  • Detailed description of each work item
  • Unit of measurement (m¬≥, m¬≤, LM, No....)
  • Measured quantity
  • Unit rate (filled by contractor)
  • Total amount for each item

üìè Common Units of Measurement

  • m¬≥ (Cubic Meter): For concrete, excavation, backfill
  • m¬≤ (Square Meter): For flooring, painting, formwork
  • LM (Linear Meter): For pipes, cables, edges
  • kg/ton: For reinforcement and metals
  • No.: For doors, windows, units

üîÑ Stages of Quantity Take-off

  1. Receive Drawings: Ensure completeness and updates.
  2. Study Project: Understand general concept and details.
  3. Divide Works: By specialty (structural, architectural, mechanical...).
  4. Measure: Extract dimensions from drawings.
  5. Calculate: Apply engineering formulas.
  6. Compile: Organize quantities in tables.
  7. Review: Verify accuracy.

üí° Tips for Beginners

  • Start with large elements then move to details.
  • Document source of every number (sheet number, detail).
  • Check calculations using different method.
  • Learn to read engineering drawings accurately.
