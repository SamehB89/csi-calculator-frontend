Choosing between Lump Sum and Unit Price contracts is a critical decision affecting risk allocation, payment method, and change management throughout the project lifecycle. Understanding their fundamental differences helps owners and contractors make the right choice.

üìã Lump Sum Contract (Fixed Price)

Definition:

A contract that commits the contractor to execute all project works for a fixed, predetermined total amount. This amount only changes through formal change orders.

How It Works:

  • Contractor submits an all-inclusive total price for all works.
  • Payment is made in installments based on completion percentage or milestones.
  • Any cost overrun is borne by the contractor.
  • Any savings belong to the contractor.

When to Use:

‚úÖ When drawings and specifications are 100% complete
‚úÖ Few scope changes are expected
‚úÖ Owner wants to know final cost upfront
‚úÖ Traditional building projects with complete design

Definition:

A contract where a price is set for each work unit (e.g., $/m¬≥ concrete or $/LM pipes). Actual executed quantities are measured and payment is based on them.

How It Works:

  • Contractor submits a schedule of unit rates for each item.
  • Actual executed quantities are measured periodically.
  • Payment = Actual Quantity √ó Unit Rate.
  • Final cost is determined after work completion.

When to Use:

‚úÖ When quantities are not precisely defined
‚úÖ Excavation and infrastructure projects
‚úÖ Owner accepts flexibility in final cost
‚úÖ Maintenance and repair projects
