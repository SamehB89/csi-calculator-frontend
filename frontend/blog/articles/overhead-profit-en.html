Overhead and Profit in Construction Pricing

January 7, 2026 10 min read

üí∞ Understanding Cost Structure

When pricing any project, it's not enough to calculate only material, labor, and equipment costs. There are "hidden" costs that must be covered, plus a reasonable profit margin. Ignoring these elements is the main reason small contractors fail.

üìä Direct vs Indirect Costs

Direct Costs

Directly linked to a specific BOQ item:

  • Raw materials (cement, steel, bricks)
  • Direct execution labor
  • Equipment rental used for the item
  • Subcontractors for specific works

Indirect Costs (Overhead)

Not linked to a specific item but necessary for project operation:

  • Management salaries (Project Manager, Accountant)
  • Site office rental
  • Utilities (electricity, water, communications)
  • Insurance and bank guarantees
  • Safety equipment

üè¢ Types of Overhead

1. Site Overhead

Costs of operating the work site:

  • Site offices and temporary storage
  • Security and guards
  • Testing and measurement equipment
  • Internal site transportation

Typical Rate: 5-8% of direct costs

2. Home Office Overhead

Costs of operating the parent company:

  • Senior management salaries
  • Headquarters rental
  • Marketing and public relations
  • Software and licenses

Typical Rate: 3-5% of direct costs

üìà Calculating Profit Margin

Profit margin depends on several factors:

  • Competition Level: Competitive market = lower profit
  • Risks: Complex project = higher profit
  • Client Relationship: Repeat work may justify lower profit
  • Project Size: Large projects accept lower percentages
